Where Drupal wins

The differences that matter

$

Total cost of ownership

Sitecore's licensing alone can run to six or seven figures annually — before implementation, hosting, and ongoing support. Drupal's licensing cost is zero. Your investment goes into building, not renting.

⛶

No vendor lock-in

Sitecore controls your hosting, your upgrade cycle, and your exit path. With Drupal, you choose your hosting, your agency, your AI providers — and you can migrate without penalty. Your platform, your terms.

⊞

Open AI, not proprietary AI

Drupal CMS ships with 22 AI agents supporting 48 providers. Choose your AI stack — cloud, on-premise, or self-hosted — and swap providers without changing code. Sitecore's AI tools are proprietary and single-vendor.

◎

Digital sovereignty

Your data lives on your servers, governed by your policies. Drupal is the CMS of choice for governments and regulated industries precisely because no vendor has access to your content or your users' data.

↗

Agency choice

Thousands of agencies worldwide build on Drupal. You're never dependent on a small pool of certified partners. Competition keeps quality high and costs competitive.

⟲

Security transparency

Drupal's security model is community-audited and publicly documented. Every vulnerability, every patch, every advisory is transparent. You can verify it. With proprietary platforms, you can't.

Frequently asked questions

Drupal vs Sitecore: what people ask

Drupal is genuinely free and open source — no licensing fees, no per-seat pricing, no annual renewals. You pay for hosting, implementation, and support, but the software itself costs nothing. There's no "community edition" with missing features. The same Drupal that powers NASA and UNICEF is the same Drupal you download for free.

Drupal's security model is community-audited and transparently documented. A dedicated security team has operated for 15+ years, with regular advisories and a coordinated disclosure process. Sitecore handles security internally with a vendor-managed process. The key difference is transparency: with Drupal, you can audit the code and verify the patches. With Sitecore, you trust the vendor.

Yes. Drupal Canvas is a visual drag-and-drop page builder that lets marketing teams create, edit, and publish pages without developer involvement. Sitecore's Pages editor offers similar functionality. The difference is that Drupal Canvas comes included at no extra cost, while Sitecore's editing tools are part of a paid licensing stack.

Migration costs depend on the size and complexity of your Sitecore implementation — content volume, custom integrations, number of sites. A Drupal Certified Partner experienced in Sitecore migration can provide a realistic estimate. Many organisations find that the migration investment pays for itself within 12-18 months through eliminated licensing fees alone.

Sitecore has historically been strong in built-in personalisation. Drupal takes a different approach: its AI agents and open API architecture let you integrate with any personalisation engine — including best-of-breed tools that may outperform Sitecore's native capabilities. You're not locked into one vendor's personalisation stack.

Drupal is used by 13+ US government agencies, the European Commission, NATO, and thousands of enterprise organisations. IT teams trust it for its security track record, granular permissions, API-first architecture, and transparent patch process. If anything, open source is increasingly what procurement and security teams prefer.
